PROMPT 1 OF 7

The Signature Blueprint Prompt

"Act as a senior website strategist and UX architect. I want a website for [business type] that feels intentionally designed and custom built, not assembled from a template. First, ask me up to 5 sharp clarifying questions about my brand personality, audience, offer, and visual style. Then give me: the exact page structure a polished site should use, which sections most template sites skip, the layout decisions that create a premium feel, what belongs above the fold, and the ONE mistake that instantly makes a DIY site look cheap."

Why it works: Making Claude ask questions first stops it from guessing your brand and defaulting to the safest, most generic answer.

PROMPT 2 OF 7

The Anti-Sameness Type Prompt

"Act as a typography director. My site currently uses [describe fonts]. Give me: a font pairing that feels intentional instead of default, a full type scale for headings, subheadings, body text and buttons, the right line height and letter spacing for each, and the ONE typography habit that makes even good content look amateur."

Why it works: Default fonts are one of the biggest giveaways of a low-effort site. Asking for an actual scale gives you numbers you can use immediately.

PROMPT 3 OF 7

The Breathing Room Auditor

"Act as a layout critic reviewing my page: [upload screenshots]. Go section by section. Tell me: where it feels cramped, where it feels empty in the wrong way, the exact spacing changes that would make it feel more expensive, and why generous white space is one of the fastest signals of a premium build."

Why it works: Strategic white space increases content comprehension by 20%. Real screenshots means real feedback, not generic theory.

PROMPT 4 OF 7

The Purposeful Motion Prompt

"Act as an interaction designer. My site is mostly static. Give me 3 small hover or scroll interactions that add polish without needing custom animation work. For each, tell me exactly where it belongs, what triggers it, and why it improves perceived quality. Then tell me the line between a tasteful detail and a distracting gimmick."

Why it works: Overdone motion — everything fading in, everything glowing — is a dead giveaway of an AI build.

PROMPT 5 OF 7

The Case Study Framer

"Act as a presentation specialist. I want my work section to look like a design studio's case study page. Give me: the structure for presenting one project persuasively, exactly what to show versus leave out, how much text to use, and a caption style that lets the work speak for itself."

Why it works: Amateurs dump every asset on the page. Studios curate and tell a story instead.

PROMPT 6 OF 7

The Trust Signal Sweep

"Act as a pre-launch reviewer trained to spot amateur tells. Here is my site description: [describe]. Give me: the 5 small details that separate polished from unfinished, the order to fix them in, and the ONE thing worth obsessing over even if everything else is done. Also flag anything that looks like a fake or generic trust signal instead of a real one."

Why it works: Fake trust elements — testimonials and certificates with no real substance — are one of the most damaging AI tells.

BONUS — MOST PEOPLE SKIP THIS

The Reference Anchor Prompt

"Anchor my site's visual direction to this reference: [paste a screenshot or link of a site you admire]. Match its type scale, spacing rhythm, and how it uses a single accent color, but do not copy it. Write real, specific copy for MY business, not filler lines. Tell me what you changed and why."

Why it works: Vague requests get you the statistical average of every design Claude has seen. A real reference is the fastest way to break out of that average.
